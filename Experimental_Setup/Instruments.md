## Measurement Instruments

Electrical characterization of HfO₂-based MOS capacitors was performed using standard
laboratory instruments suitable for precise C–V and I–V measurements. The instruments
used in this project are commonly employed in semiconductor research and device
characterization.

---

### 1. Source Measure Unit (SMU)

A precision Source Measure Unit (SMU) was used for current–voltage (I–V) measurements.

**Purpose:**
- Apply controlled voltage across the MOS capacitor
- Measure resulting leakage current with high accuracy

**Key features:**
- Wide voltage and current measurement range
- High sensitivity for low-current measurements
- Suitable for analyzing leakage current and tunneling behavior

The SMU enables reliable identification of conduction mechanisms such as Poole–Frenkel
emission and Fowler–Nordheim tunneling.

---

### 2. LCR Meter / Capacitance Measurement Unit

An LCR meter was used to perform capacitance–voltage (C–V) measurements.

**Purpose:**
- Measure capacitance and conductance as a function of applied bias
- Evaluate electrostatic properties of MOS capacitors

**Extracted parameters:**
- Oxide capacitance
- Flat-band voltage
- Interface trap density
- Oxide charge density

C–V analysis provides critical information regarding interface quality and dielectric
performance.

---

### 3. Probe Station

A probe station was used to establish electrical contact with the MOS capacitor samples.

**Purpose:**
- Precise probing of metal contacts on fabricated devices
- Stable and repeatable electrical measurements

**Key components:**
- Micro-positioned probes
- Chuck for sample placement
- Shielded environment to minimize noise

The probe station ensures accurate and reproducible measurements during both C–V and I–V
characterization.

---

### 4. Temperature Control (If Applicable)

For selected measurements, temperature conditions were controlled to study temperature
dependent electrical behavior.

**Purpose:**
- Analyze temperature dependence of leakage current
- Support identification of thermally activated conduction mechanisms

Temperature-controlled measurements help differentiate between field-driven and
thermally assisted conduction processes.

---

### 5. Data Acquisition and Analysis Tools

Measurement data were recorded and analyzed using standard data acquisition and plotting
tools.

**Purpose:**
- Store experimental measurement data
- Generate C–V, I–V, FN, and PF plots
- Perform model fitting and comparative analysis

Data analysis enables correlation between fabrication parameters and electrical behavior.

---

### Summary

The combination of precision measurement instruments and controlled experimental setup
enabled reliable electrical characterization of HfO₂ MOS capacitors. Familiarity with
these instruments demonstrates practical experience in semiconductor device
characterization and experimental analysis.
